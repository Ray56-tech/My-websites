01 — SURVEILLANCE

THEY ARE READING THIS WITH YOU

The most comprehensive surveillance system in human history was not built by a dystopian regime in a science fiction novel. It was built by democracies. And it covers you.

In 2013, NSA contractor Edward Snowden leaked documents proving that the United States had built a global surveillance infrastructure capable of intercepting virtually all digital communications on earth. The programme — called PRISM — collected data directly from the servers of Apple, Google, Facebook, Microsoft, Yahoo, and others. Not with warrants. Systematically.

The Five Eyes alliance — the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand — share this surveillance data with each other, legally circumventing their own domestic laws. If US law prevents the NSA from spying on its own citizens, Britain's GCHQ does it instead, and shares the data. Every country does this for every other. It is fully documented.

The NSA's XKeyscore programme allowed analysts to search through the private emails, online chats, and browsing histories of millions of people worldwide with no prior authorisation. An analyst simply had to fill out a form justifying the search — which was never reviewed before the search was conducted. This is confirmed from Snowden's leaked documents.

Pegasus spyware — developed by Israeli surveillance company NSO Group — has been used by governments across the Middle East, Africa, and Europe to spy on journalists, human rights activists, opposition politicians, and lawyers. Once installed on a phone, it gives operators complete access: messages, emails, calls, camera, microphone, location. All in real time. It was used to track associates of murdered journalist Jamal Khashoggi before his death.

Your phone is the most powerful surveillance device ever built — and you carry it voluntarily. Every app you install is a potential data collection node. Your location, your contacts, your browsing habits, your voice — all of it is being stored somewhere by someone. This is not a theory. It is the documented business model of the global technology industry.

China's Social Credit System is the most advanced implementation of surveillance-as-governance ever attempted. Cameras with facial recognition track citizens in public spaces. Online behaviour, purchases, social connections, and stated opinions are all fed into a score that determines whether you can travel, take out loans, or send your children to certain schools. As of 2024 versions of this system are being studied and partially adopted by governments across the world.

You are not the customer of the internet. You are the product. Your attention, your data, your behaviour — sold continuously to anyone who pays.
